Overview

This project focuses on designing and simulating an optimized multi-elevator control system using MATLAB. The objective was to improve passenger travel time and energy efficiency in a multi-story building environment. The system implements intelligent scheduling algorithms to minimize wait times and optimize elevator dispatching based on real-time demand patterns. Through comprehensive simulations, the optimized control strategy demonstrated significant improvements in both operational efficiency and user experience compared to traditional elevator control methods.

Key Features

  • Intelligent elevator scheduling algorithm for multi-car systems
  • Real-time demand pattern analysis and prediction
  • Energy consumption optimization strategies
  • Comprehensive MATLAB simulation environment
  • Performance metrics tracking (wait time, travel time, energy usage)
  • Comparison with traditional control strategies

Technologies Used

  • MATLAB/Simulink
  • Control Systems Design
  • Optimization Algorithms
  • Simulation and Modeling
  • Performance Analysis Tools

Results

The optimized control system successfully reduced average passenger wait times by minimizing unnecessary elevator movements and implementing predictive dispatching. Energy efficiency was improved through strategic car allocation and idle state management. The simulation results validate the effectiveness of the proposed optimization approach for real-world elevator control applications.
